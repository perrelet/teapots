From Blyton's *Old Thatch* series (the title story of its first booklet, *The
Talking Teapot and Other Tales*, 1934): a pixie, Dimble-Dumble, finds a yellow
talking teapot in a gorse bush and carries it home to replace his own cracked one
— though he suspects it really belongs to a Yellow Gnome. A minor, mischievous
magical object whose voice drives a short children's tale. Notable here for what
it *lacks*: agency and anthropomorphism, yes, but **no character arc** — a useful
data point that `char:arc` is not universal to teapot-characters.
