Orwell's 1946 essay lays down eleven "golden rules" for making tea, several of them
emphatic teapot doctrine: tea must be brewed *in a teapot* (never an urn or
cauldron), the pot china or earthenware, warmed first, the leaves spooned straight
in without a strainer. Wry but wholly earnest — the teapot is the load-bearing
object of the argument. A `discursive` entry beside [[goddess-in-a-teapot]]: the
teapot as something a writer thinks and prescribes *through*.
