The Los Angeles ceramicist — a Memphis Group figure — has made bold, brightly
geometric, often barely-functional teapots since the 1970s, calling the teapot a
"social modifier": an object whose real work is to shift how people gather and
relate. `formal-study` shading into `hospitality`: the teapot as sculpture that
still remembers it belongs to a shared table.
