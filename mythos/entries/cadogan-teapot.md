A teapot with **no lid**: it is filled upside-down through a hole in its base,
where an internal cone tube traps the liquid when the pot is righted — a gravity
trick that makes filling look impossible. First made around 1830 at Rockingham,
copied from a Chinese peach-shaped wine pot in the Earl of Cadogan's collection,
and a staple of museum ceramics ever since. The exemplar of the `mechanism` mode
and a natural sibling to the [[assassin-teapot]]: two trick-vessels, queryable
together by the `trick-vessel` tag.
