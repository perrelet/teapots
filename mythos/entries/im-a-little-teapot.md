Written in 1939 for young children who couldn't master a tap routine: a song
simple enough that the child doesn't sing *about* a teapot but *becomes* one — arm
out for the spout, arm bent for the handle, then "tip me over and pour me out."
The corpus's only `performative` entry and one of two that *name themselves*
("I'm a little teapot" / HTTP 418's "I'm a teapot" — linked by the shared
`self-naming` tag, not an edge). Dismissed as inane novelty in 1941; it has
outlasted almost everything published beside it — the chocolate-teapot pattern
again. `spout: yes`: the child's own arm.
