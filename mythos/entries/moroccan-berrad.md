The engraved silver or brass Maghrebi teapot (*berrad*), with its long curved
spout, poured from a foot or more above the glass to raise a frothy "crown" — the
centrepiece of Moroccan hospitality, where the height and grace of the pour encode
welcome and respect for the guest. A `spout: yes` entry in the strongest sense:
the whole ritual lives in the arc of tea leaving the spout. The Maghrebi
counterpart to [[yixing-gongfu]] — two ceremonies where the teapot is revered
through the act of pouring.
