A ~250-lb bronze teapot mounted atop an upright Revolutionary-War cannon barrel
before the 1767 Chowan County Courthouse in Edenton, North Carolina,
commemorating the Edenton Tea Party of 25 October 1774 — when fifty-one women,
led by Penelope Barker, signed a public resolution to boycott British tea and
cloth, and signed by *name* rather than in disguise. One of the earliest
organised political actions by women in the colonies, ten months *after* the
Boston Tea Party. The bronze was cast in 1905 by foundryman Frank Baldwin. Here
the `effigy` carries not scandal but resistance: the teapot as monument to
women's political voice.
