A 2011 viral photo craze: strike the "I'm a Little Teapot" pose — one hand on the
hip for the handle, the other arm out for the spout — and photograph it in unlikely
places. Started by two Australian teachers as a deliberately sillier, safer answer
to planking, it briefly swept the internet. The `alludes-to` edge to
[[im-a-little-teapot]] is exact: teapotting is the song's pantomime detached from
the nursery and performed by adults for the camera — the same `embodiment` and
`self-naming`, one register over.
