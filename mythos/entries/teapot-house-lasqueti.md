An off-grid house on Lasqueti Island, British Columbia, named "The Teapot House"
for the brick fireplace chimney shaped like a teapot (a matching coffee-pot
chimney once stood over the kitchen stove, lost in a re-roofing). Built in the
1920s by George Hadley, across a century it has been pioneer homestead, hippie
commune, restaurant and music venue, and remains the island's gathering place —
the traditional site of weddings, funerals, the firefighters' picnic and
Halloween. Its people are known as the **Teapotters**: the teapot adopted not as
doctrine but as the emblem of a whole communal way of life — `effigy` grown into
`hospitality` and `veneration`.
