Yixing teapots, thrown from unglazed zisha ("purple sand") clay, are said to
absorb the tea over years of use, seasoning the vessel until it flavours plain
water — so a well-kept pot is dedicated to a single tea and never washed with
soap. In Gongfu practice the teapot is a revered instrument of attention: heat,
time, and care turned into communion. `veneration` and `hospitality` at once —
the sacred lives in the ceremony and the seasoned object together.
