A porcelain teapot modelled as a limp Aesthetic figure — languid youth on one
side, maiden on the other — inscribed "Fearful Consequences Through the Laws of
Natural Selection & Evolution of Living up to One's Teapot." It skewers Oscar
Wilde's "I find it harder and harder every day to live up to my blue china":
Hadley takes the joke to its literal end — devotees of beauty have *become*
teapots. The corpus's clearest `satire` object, and quietly profound about how the
objects we prize reshape us.
